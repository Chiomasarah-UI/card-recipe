VEGETABLE YAM

Yams originated in Africa, Asia, and the Caribbean. Africans call yams “nyami,” which is where we get the word “yam.” They are cylindrical and vary in size. Some of the largest yams have weighed more than 100 pounds and have been several feet long. Yams have a dense white, purple, or red flesh and scaly brown skin with dark spots. A yam is starchy and dry. In African culture, it is typically boiled or roasted.Vegetable yam (ji akwukwo) is a dish made with boiled yam, palm oil and vegetables, mostly green amaranth. You can also use a combination of two vegetables.

INGREDIENTS

  • 300g yam
  • 40g ukpaka
  • 40g onions, 1/4 cup chopped onions
  • scotch bonnet, chopped
  • 1 seasoning cube
  • 1/4 cup palm oil
  • 210g green amaranth
  • 30g ariral vegetable (optional)
  • salt

PREPARATIONS

  1. Cut and peel yam, wash and cut into 2-inch cubes.
  2. Chop onions and pepper.
  3. Pick and wash green amaranth and arirala. Shred finely.
  4. Place yam cubes in a pot, add enough water to cover it, add a little salt and some chopped onions.
  5. Boil until yam is soft and a fork passes through easily
  6. Drain using a sieve and set the yam aside.
  7. Roughly crush the ukpaka to smaller bits, this makes it act as both a flavouring agent and a component of the dish.
  8. Place the yam back into the empty pot, add ukpaka, onions, pepper and seasoning cube.
  9. Stir very well using a wooden spoon.
  10. Taste and add salt and palm oil.
  11. Place over low heat.
  12. Add the shredded vegetables
  13. Stir and cover, leave to steam till vegetables have softened but not mushy.
  14. Serve hot.
